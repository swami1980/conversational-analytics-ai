# Recruiting Glossary

## Requisition (Req)
A requisition is an approved job opening. It represents a headcount slot that has been budgeted and approved for hiring. Each req has a job family, level, location, org, team, recruiter, and hiring manager. A req can be Open (actively hiring), Closed (filled or cancelled), or On Hold (paused). Reqs are tracked by how many days they have been open and whether they have a Bar Raiser requirement.

## Candidate
A candidate is a person who has applied for or been sourced for a specific requisition. Each candidate moves through pipeline stages from Applied all the way to Offer Accepted or Declined. Candidates are linked to a single req and assigned to a recruiter and hiring manager.

## Pipeline
The pipeline refers to the collection of candidates currently in progress for a requisition or across all reqs. Pipeline health is measured by how many candidates are in each stage and how long they have been in the process.

## Pipeline Stage
The stage a candidate is currently in. Stages in order are: Applied, Phone Screen Scheduled, Phone Screen Completed, Onsite Scheduled, Onsite Completed, Offer Extended, Offer Accepted, Offer Declined, Withdrawn.

## Pending Start
A pending start is a candidate who has accepted an offer but has not yet joined the company. They have a confirmed start date and are going through pre-boarding. Key tracking points include whether their laptop has been provisioned and whether their badge has been requested.

## Onboarding Readiness
A measure of whether a pending start is fully prepared to join on their start date. A pending start is considered at risk if their laptop has not been provisioned or their badge has not been requested within 30 days of their start date.

## Bar Raiser
A Bar Raiser is an independently trained interviewer who is not part of the hiring team. Their role is to ensure the candidate raises the bar for the overall talent level. Bar Raiser interviews are a required step for certain job families including SDE, SDM, Applied Scientist, and Research Scientist. The Bar Raiser has veto power over a hire decision.

## Time to Hire
The number of days between when a requisition was opened and when an offer was accepted. Average time to hire is a key efficiency metric tracked by job family and org.

## Offer Acceptance Rate
The percentage of offers extended that are accepted by candidates. A low offer acceptance rate may indicate compensation issues, competing offers, or candidate experience problems.

## Conversion Rate
The percentage of candidates who move from one pipeline stage to the next. Common conversion rates tracked are screen to onsite, onsite to offer, and offer to hire.

## Headcount
The total number of active employees in an org, team, or location. Headcount is tracked at the start and end of each quarter, with new hires, attrition, promotions, and transfers recorded separately.

## Attrition
The rate at which employees leave the company, whether through resignation, termination, retirement, or transfer. Attrition rate is calculated as departures divided by total headcount.

## Hiring Plan
The target number of new hires approved for a given org and time period. Plan attainment measures how closely actual hiring tracked against the plan.

## Hiring Velocity
The speed and volume of hiring over a period. Measured as total hires versus hiring plan, often tracked quarter by quarter.

## Job Family
The category of role being hired. Job families in this system include SDE (Software Development Engineer), SDM (Software Development Manager), TPM (Technical Program Manager), SDET (Software Development Engineer in Test), Data Engineer, Applied Scientist, Research Scientist, Solutions Architect, and DevOps Engineer.

## Level
The seniority level of a role, ranging from L4 (entry-level professional) to L7 (principal/distinguished). SDEs go from L4 to L7, SDMs from L6 to L7.

## Sourcing Channel
Where a candidate came from. Channels tracked are External (job boards, direct apply), Internal (existing employee applying), Agency (third-party recruiter), and Referral (employee referral).

## Loop
A full onsite interview process consisting of multiple back-to-back interview sessions covering different competencies. A loop typically takes half a day and includes a Bar Raiser session for applicable roles.

## Feedback SLA
The expectation that interviewers submit written feedback within 24 hours of completing an interview. Missing feedback delays the hiring decision and is tracked as a compliance metric.
