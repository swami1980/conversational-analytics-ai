# How to Use the Recruiting Analytics AI

## Asking Questions
Type your question in plain English in the chat box and press Ask. You do not need to use any special syntax or know which system holds the data. The AI figures that out automatically.

Good question formats:
- "How many open SDE L5 reqs do we have in Seattle?"
- "Which candidates are closest to an offer right now?"
- "What is the offer acceptance rate for AWS this year?"
- "Show me the Bar Raiser schedule for the next two weeks"
- "Are there any pending starts without a laptop provisioned?"
- "Give me a recruiting health check for Prime Video"
- "Which orgs are behind on their hiring plan this quarter?"

## Follow-Up Questions
After each answer, the AI suggests 3 follow-up questions based on the conversation. Click any chip to ask that question immediately. You can also type your own follow-up — the AI remembers the last 15 exchanges so you can refer to previous answers naturally.

## Understanding the Agent Reasoning Panel
The right side of the screen shows the AI's reasoning in real time. You can see:
- Which knowledge base entries it looked up to find the right API
- Which APIs it called and with what parameters
- A preview of the raw data it received before formatting

This lets you verify exactly where each answer came from.

## Roles and What You Can See
- **Recruiter** — full view of all reqs, candidates, and metrics across every org
- **Hiring Manager** — your own reqs and candidates only, filtered automatically
- **Admin** — full access to everything including system-level information

## What the AI Cannot Do
- It cannot make changes to any data — it is read-only
- It cannot access candidate resumes or documents
- It cannot send emails or schedule interviews
- It does not have access to compensation or offer letter details

## When Something Looks Wrong
If the AI returns unexpected results, check whether:
- Your question is specific enough (add org, level, or time period)
- The data exists in the system for the criteria you specified
- You are logged in with the right role

You can ask the AI to explain its answer or retry with different parameters.

## Sessions
Each conversation is a session. Your last 15 exchanges are remembered within a session, giving the AI context for follow-up questions. Start a new session using the New Session button in the top right when you want a fresh conversation.
